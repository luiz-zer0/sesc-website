About Us

The Strathmore Environmental and Sustainability Community (SESC) is a student-led initiative committed to promoting environmental awareness, sustainable living, and climate action within the university and the larger community. The club is passionate about making a positive impact through practical activities such as tree planting drives, which not only beautify our surroundings but also contribute to global reforestation efforts.

In addition to hands-on projects, SESC places a strong emphasis on education. The club regularly invites guest speakers—ranging from environmental experts to climate activists—to engage students in thought-provoking discussions on climate change, environmental policy, and sustainability solutions. Through these initiatives, SESC empowers students to become informed and active stewards of the environment.

Vision

We aim to Raise awareness about climate change, waste reduction, and sustainable living through campaigns, workshops, and educational programs. Promote sustainable practices on campus by encouraging recycling, energy conservation, responsible consumption, and eco-friendly transportation. Support student-led projects that address environmental challenges within the university and surrounding communities. Collaborate with university departments and external organizations to implement green policies and initiatives. Create inclusive spaces for dialogue, innovation, and action toward achieving a low-carbon, environmentally just future.

Mission

Engage students in impactful environmental initiatives and educational programs that inspire action, build awareness, and cultivate long-term commitment to sustainability. We strive to Empower students to take the lead in planning and implementing eco-friendly projects such as tree planting, clean-up drives, waste reduction campaigns, and green innovations. Provide hands-on learning opportunities through workshops, sustainability challenges, field visits, and eco-competitions that connect theory with real-world environmental solutions. Integrate environmental education into student life by collaborating with faculty to support research, host guest lectures, and promote interdisciplinary learning around climate and ecology.
